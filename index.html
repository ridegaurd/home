Real Life Testing Scenarios

Sudden Skid on Wet Road

1. Loss of Control When a rider suddenly loses balance due to slippery roads, sudden braking, or evasive maneuvers, RideGuard should detect the instability and trigger its safety mechanisms. The goal is to minimize injury by cushioning impact points and alerting nearby vehicles if necessary. Test Points: Simulate sudden skids on wet/sandy surfaces. Sudden braking at high speed. Sharp cornering with lean-angle instability. Emergency swerve around an obstacle.

Nighttime Collision

Riding at night poses higher risks due to reduced visibility for both the rider and others on the road. RideGuard’s sensors and visibility-enhancing features (like reflective alerts or auto-triggered lights) should engage during a crash or near-miss. Test Points: Simulated head-on collision at night with poor street lighting. Side-impact from another vehicle at an intersection. Rear-end collision test with RideGuard’s warning system. Low-speed fall on dark, uneven roads.

Off-Road Accident

When riding on trails, gravel paths, or unpaved roads, accidents can occur due to uneven terrain, loose soil, or unexpected obstacles. RideGuard should withstand harsher conditions and still activate effectively. Test Points: Sudden slip on gravel while ascending/descending a slope. Collision with rocks, tree branches, or uneven ground. Low-speed tumble on muddy trails. Drop test on rocky surfaces to check durability and activation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does RideGuard detect accidents?
RideGuard uses a combination of gyroscopic sensors, accelerometers, and AI algorithms to detect abnormal movements and impacts. The system can distinguish between normal riding maneuvers and potential accidents with 99.7% accuracy.
How long does the protection system last?
The standard battery provides approximately 30 hours of continuous use. The system automatically enters low-power mode when not in motion, extending battery life to about 2 weeks between charges. The air cushions maintain pressure for up to 24 hours after deployment.
Is RideGuard compatible with all types of two-wheelers?
Yes! RideGuard is designed to work with all motorcycles, scooters, and e-bikes. The universal mounting system adapts to different vehicle frames, and the protective gear is adjustable for all rider sizes.
How much maintenance does RideGuard require?
RideGuard requires minimal maintenance. We recommend a monthly system check through the mobile app, which performs a full diagnostic. The air canisters are replaceable after deployment and have a 5-year shelf life. The outer shell can be wiped clean with a damp cloth.
What happens if I have a minor fall that doesn't require deployment?
RideGuard's AI is trained to distinguish between minor incidents and serious accidents. For minor falls, the system will alert you to check for damage but won't deploy the protection. You can always manually trigger the emergency alert if needed.
